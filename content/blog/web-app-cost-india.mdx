If you're planning to build a web application in India, one of your first questions is probably: "How much will this cost?"

The honest answer is: it depends. A simple business website and a full SaaS platform are very different projects. But I can give you real numbers based on what agencies and developers actually charge in 2026.

This guide breaks down everything you need to know about web development pricing in India. Whether you're a startup founder, a small business owner, or a company looking to build internal tools, you'll walk away with realistic budget expectations.

## Why Web Development Prices Vary So Much

Two developers can quote Rs 50,000 and Rs 5,00,000 for what sounds like the same project. Here's why:

**Scope and Complexity**: A 5-page website is not the same as a 50-screen web application with user authentication, payment processing, and admin dashboards. The more features you need, the higher the cost.

**Technology Stack**: A WordPress site costs less than a custom React + NestJS application because of development time and complexity. Modern JavaScript frameworks require more expertise and take longer to build.

**Team Structure**: A freelancer working alone charges differently than an agency with project managers, QA testers, and multiple developers. Agencies have higher overheads but offer more structure and accountability.

**Timeline Requirements**: Rush projects cost more. If you need something in 2 weeks instead of 8, expect a 30-50% premium. Good work takes time, and compressing timelines means reshuffling priorities.

**Experience Level**: A developer with 8 years of experience building SaaS platforms will charge more than someone just starting out. You're paying for expertise, fewer mistakes, and faster delivery.

## Breaking Down Project Types

### Business Websites (Rs 25,000 – Rs 75,000)

A standard business website includes 5-10 pages: home, about, services, contact, and maybe a blog or portfolio section. It's primarily informational and designed to establish credibility online.

At the lower end (Rs 25,000-40,000), you're looking at template-based designs with basic customization. At the higher end, you get custom designs, animations, and better SEO optimization.

### WordPress Sites (Rs 40,000 – Rs 1,00,000)

WordPress powers over 40% of the web. It's ideal for content-heavy sites, blogs, and businesses that need to update content frequently without developer help.

The cost includes theme customization, plugin setup, and training. Custom WordPress themes with ACF (Advanced Custom Fields) and custom post types push toward the higher end of this range.

### E-Commerce Stores (Rs 75,000 – Rs 2,00,000)

Setting up a Shopify or WooCommerce store involves product configuration, payment gateway integration (Razorpay, PayU, Paytm), shipping calculator setup, and often custom theme work.

A basic store with 50-100 products starts around Rs 75,000. Stores with custom features like subscription boxes, complex inventory management, or multi-vendor capabilities can exceed Rs 2,00,000.

### Custom Web Applications (Rs 1,00,000 – Rs 5,00,000)

This is where things get interesting. Custom web applications include dashboards, admin panels, CRM systems, booking platforms, and internal business tools.

These projects typically use modern frameworks like React or Vue.js on the frontend and Node.js or NestJS on the backend. They require proper architecture, database design, and often third-party integrations.

### SaaS Platforms (Rs 2,00,000 – Rs 10,00,000+)

Building a SaaS product is a significant investment. You're creating a multi-tenant application that handles user authentication, subscription billing, data isolation, and potentially thousands of users.

An MVP (Minimum Viable Product) starts around Rs 2,00,000-3,00,000. A production-ready SaaS with all the bells and whistles can easily exceed Rs 10,00,000.

## Agency vs Freelancer: Which Should You Choose?

**Freelancers** (Rs 1,500 – Rs 4,000/hour) are cheaper but may lack bandwidth for larger projects. You get one person's skills and availability. If they get sick or have other commitments, your project stalls.

Freelancers work best for:
- Small projects under Rs 1,00,000
- Well-defined scope with clear requirements
- When you have technical knowledge to manage the project

**Agencies** (Rs 2,500 – Rs 6,000/hour) cost more but bring teams, project management, and accountability. For mission-critical projects, the structure matters.

Agencies work best for:
- Projects over Rs 2,00,000
- Complex requirements that may evolve
- When you need ongoing maintenance and support
- When the project is business-critical

## What Drives Cost Up

Several factors can push your project into higher price brackets:

**Custom Design**: Templates save money but limit uniqueness. Custom designs require a designer, multiple revision rounds, and more development time.

**User Authentication and Roles**: Adding login functionality with different user types (admin, manager, customer) adds significant complexity. Secure authentication isn't trivial.

**Payment Integration**: Connecting Razorpay, Stripe, or other payment gateways requires careful implementation and testing. Handling edge cases like failed payments, refunds, and webhooks takes time.

**Third-Party API Integrations**: Connecting to CRMs, shipping providers, accounting software, or other services each add development hours and potential maintenance burden.

**Real-Time Features**: Chat, live notifications, and real-time dashboards require WebSocket implementations and more complex architecture.

**Admin Dashboards**: Every user-facing feature often needs a corresponding admin interface. This can effectively double your feature set.

**AI Features**: Chatbots, content generation, and AI-powered search are increasingly common. These require API integrations and careful prompt engineering.

**Mobile Responsiveness**: This is table stakes today. But complex layouts that work beautifully on desktop need significant work to function on mobile.

## What to Budget for Your Project

If you're a startup or small business, here's a realistic budget guide:

**Basic Online Presence**: Rs 30,000 – Rs 50,000. This gets you a professional website that establishes credibility. No complex functionality, just solid design and SEO basics.

**E-Commerce Store**: Rs 75,000 – Rs 1,50,000. A functional online store with payment processing, inventory management, and a design that doesn't look like a template.

**Custom Business Tool**: Rs 1,00,000 – Rs 3,00,000. An internal tool, customer portal, or specialized application tailored to your business processes.

**MVP for a SaaS**: Rs 2,00,000 – Rs 5,00,000. The minimum viable product to test your idea with real users and get feedback before building the full vision.

**Full Product Launch**: Rs 5,00,000+. A complete, production-ready product with all features, proper testing, and documentation.

Budget 10-20% extra for post-launch fixes and improvements. No software launches perfectly, and you'll want runway for iterations based on user feedback.

## Hidden Costs to Consider

The development quote isn't the total cost of ownership. Factor in:

**Hosting**: Rs 500 – Rs 10,000/month depending on traffic and requirements. Vercel and Railway have good free tiers for starting out.

**Domain**: Rs 500 – Rs 5,000/year depending on the domain extension.

**SSL Certificate**: Free with Let's Encrypt, but some enterprises require extended validation certificates (Rs 5,000-15,000/year).

**Third-Party Services**: Email marketing, analytics, error tracking, and other SaaS tools add up. Budget Rs 2,000-10,000/month for a typical setup.

**Maintenance**: Ongoing updates, security patches, and small changes. Plan for Rs 5,000-15,000/month or get a maintenance retainer.

## How to Get an Accurate Quote

The best way to get a real number is to talk to developers or agencies with your specific requirements. Before reaching out, prepare:

**Write down what you need**: Features, pages, and integrations you require. Be specific. "User dashboard" means different things to different people.

**Know your timeline**: When do you need this live? Is the deadline flexible? Rush work costs more.

**Set a budget range**: This helps filter out mismatches early. If your budget is Rs 50,000 and the project realistically costs Rs 2,00,000, it's better to know that upfront.

**Gather examples**: Sites or apps that do something similar to what you want. Visual references save hours of explanation.

**Identify must-haves vs nice-to-haves**: What's essential for launch versus what can come later? This helps prioritize if the budget is tight.

## Red Flags When Getting Quotes

Watch out for:

**Quotes without discovery**: Anyone quoting a price without understanding your requirements is guessing. Good agencies ask questions before proposing.

**Unusually low prices**: If someone quotes Rs 20,000 for a custom web application, they're either underestimating scope or planning to cut corners.

**No written contract**: Every project should have a clear scope document and contract. Verbal agreements lead to disputes.

**No portfolio or references**: Ask to see similar work and talk to past clients. Experience matters.

**Unclear communication**: If they're hard to reach before the project starts, it won't improve once they have your money.
